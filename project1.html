[Redesigning the toothbrush]



Type :

BEng Individual Project

Time :

2018/2019

Location :

University of Surrey




(01)
ABOUT THE PROJECT

    This project is based on designing an oral cleansing tool to rival the current toothbrushes in the market. The approach focused on experimenting with the brush design, to improve it in the critical areas of the traditional toothbrushes.

(02)
THE PROBLEM

    Conventional toothbrushes have a lot of obvious disadvantages, mostly relating to their use of Nylon bristles and since they are used daily, it is crucial for them to be as effective as possible. It has been shown that bristle wear and contamination, seriously reduce their efficacy of brushing and in conventional toothbrushes this occurs already within a few months of use (Tangade, 2013; Frazelle, 2012).

(03)
THE SOLUTION

    The final product designed is a sonic toothbrush made of polypropylene, which utilizes a silicone brush head and unlike the conventional bristles has a semi-circular pattern. This type of brush allows it to have a higher durability, resulting in more effective brushing and also harbors less microorganisms, due to its lower total surface area.
