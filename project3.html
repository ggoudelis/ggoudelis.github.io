[Sustainable and modular kettle]



Type :

MSc Individual Project

Time :

2020

Location :

University College London




(01)
THE PROBLEM

    This project is based on redesigning the conventional kettle into a more sustainable product. The solution provided utilizes a dual chamber feature to reduce its energy consumption by preventing overfilling. It has also been designed with fully modular components to lower its overall waste output throughout its lifetime.


(02)
THE PROCCESS

    It was found by DEFRA (2008), that in the UK, 1.27 TWh of energy is wasted every year, due to overfilling of kettles. Additionally, considering an average kettles lifetime is only 4.4 years (Gallego-Schmidt, 2018), due to minor product failures, they result in a waste output that includes valuable materials such as nickel, chromium and copper.

(03)
THE SOLUTION

    The lid can be opened by pressing the button on the top of the device and then water can be filled. By using the button on the side of the chamber, water can be dispensed from the top filling chamber to the boiling chamber in a controlled rate. The base has an LCD that displays the water temperature actively and two buttons: one for the deviceâ€™s activation and one for changing the target temperature at which water will be boiled.
